Module 1: Music Time-Machine




About the Piece


The theme of the piece is nostalgia. I go back in time through music and end at the music I heard when
I was a kid because my parents played it. I then work my way to that point by passing through music
that had an immense impact on me as I was growing up. This project was especially meaningful since I'm
graduating and can't help but look back. The albums highlighted are, Take Care by Drake,
good kid, m.A.A.d city by Kendrick Lamar, 2014 Forest Hills Drive by J Cole, and The Barbra Streisand Album
by Barbra Streisand.


The Music Time Machine functions as follows: The joystick poking out of the top of the enclosure acts as a fast forward
mechanism, which moves the user further into the past from the present. Flicking the joystick to the left will play a song that
was released before than the previous one. Within the enclosure, the joystick is connected to a breadboard configured the same
way it was for joystick lab we did in class. Since I am only using the x-axis however, to move backwards through time, I only
attached the x-sensor to pin 13 on my ESP32, as well as ground and power of course.


I made several design choices to best portray my appreciation for these pieces. For one, the enclosure is made
entirely of the vinyl cases of these albums. This proved to be a good amount of work because of the height of the
cases. I ended up stacking some textbooks on top of each other so that breadboard and joystick would be tall enough
to poke through the in the case. I also used the back of the Barba Streisand album to be the lid of the enclosure
because frankly, it was a dollar and was the only one it didn't hurt too much to cut a hole in. I chose the back
because it was quite disturbing to see Barb's face with a hole in it. Moreover, I chose to start with the more
recent songs and work my way backwards as opposed to the other way around because I wanted nostalgia to increase
as the user moved through the experience. I also took the pictures in my emptied room to further accentuate the
feeling of nostalgia, the vinyls that made my enclosure were once on the now empty wall behind it.


Setup

Creating the exhibit requires a couple of steps. The first is to download the Arduino software, in my case,
I needed to download the Legacy version, an older version of the software. Once it is downloaded and running,
go to file->preferences and then enable the esp32 board by inserting this url where it says additional boards manager:
https://dl.espressif.com/dl/package_esp32_index.json

Then go to Tools->Boards->ESP32 Arduino and select TTGO T1. In order to select a port, make sure that the ESP32
is connected to the your machine using a data transfer USB-C cable. In my case, I needed to download this driver
and reset my machine in order to get the code to load on my ESP32.

For those that also need to download the driver, select the port /dev/cu.wchusbserial.

It is also required to install the TFT_eSPI library by Bodmer. This can be done by going to Tools->Library Manager.
